# Cover-letter rules

Your own rules for how cover letters should read. CoApply follows these as hard
guidance and they override its defaults where they overlap. Edit freely, add your
own, delete what you disagree with. Keep this tight — a short list of rules the
tool can actually hold in mind beats a long one it dilutes. (CoApply caps a
playbook at ~20 rules and will offer to help you consolidate if it grows past that.)

These starter rules are universal copy hygiene — they're a good default for anyone:

- Don't open by explaining the company to itself. They know their own market,
  product, and users. Open from your own specific vantage point or a concrete
  moment — not a summary of their business read back to them.
- Keep concrete proof concrete. Use the specific, quantified results from your
  positioning; don't soften them into vague "drove impact" claims. A real number,
  a real outcome, a real scope beats an adjective every time.
- Assert the positive directly. Skip the "not just X — Y" contrast mechanic;
  state what you did and who you are without the foil.
- No self-promo closers. End on a concrete, forward-looking line or let the proof
  stand. Drop "what I'm strongest at" / "where I shine" / "I'd thrive here."
- Lead with the work, not the label. Describe what you actually did and the scope
  of it, not a job title or a category.
- One story, told well, beats three name-dropped. Pick the most relevant proof
  point and give it room, rather than listing everything you've touched.
