# Cover-letter rules

Your own rules for how cover letters should read. CoApply follows these as hard
guidance and they override its defaults where they overlap. Edit freely, add your
own, delete what you disagree with.

Keep it to one rule per line (a `- ` bullet), and keep the list tight — a short
set of rules the tool can actually hold in mind beats a long one it dilutes.
CoApply caps a playbook at ~20 rules and will offer to help you consolidate past that.

These starter rules are universal copy hygiene — a good default for anyone:

- Don't open by explaining the company to itself — they know their own market; open from your own specific vantage point or a concrete moment.
- Keep concrete proof concrete: use specific, quantified results, not vague "drove impact" claims. A real number beats an adjective.
- Assert the positive directly — skip the "not just X, but Y" contrast mechanic.
- No self-promo closers — end on a concrete, forward-looking line or let the proof stand. Drop "what I'm strongest at" / "where I shine."
- Lead with the work, not the label — describe what you actually did and its scope, not a job title.
- One story told well beats three name-dropped — give your most relevant proof point room rather than listing everything.
